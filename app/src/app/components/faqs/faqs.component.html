FAQs

Brief

 Xamlinx is the first platform that allows university students, both undergraduates and graduates, to compose mock exams similar to the actual exams they took and get rewarded each time their mock exam is requested by some other students.
Practice, practice, practice...that’s what they say, right? Xamlinx is here to provide preparation material so that the real exam becomes just a second chance; and who doesn’t like second chances?

Uploads & Requests

There are two ways to upload mock exams on Xamlinx:

  • In Single Mode, students are allowed to supply mock exams up to 6 months after the real exam date.
  • In Group Mode, students may not supply a mock exam more than 72 hours after the real exam date.
Single Mode is for students who compose mock exams individually, based on their single experience with the real exam. For a Single Mode mock exam to be accepted and ultimately made available for matching to requests on our platform, the student must have scored at least 92% on the real exam.

Group Mode if for students who like to get together after an exam and discuss it. They can use the Xamlinx platform to compose a mock exam similar to the real one they took. In order for a mock exam composed in Group Mode to be accepted on our platform, the following requirements must apply:

  • At least 1 member in the group scored 92% or above
  • At least 2 members in the group scored 85% or above
  • At least 3 members in your group scored 80% or above
A group on Xamlinx must have at least 3 members in it within 24 hours of its creation date, and can have no more than 5 members.
You are encouraged to upload files for your mock exam at the time you fill out the upload form; however, you are not required to do so until you receive an email prompting you for those files. You will receive an email prompting you to upload your files when there was a matching request to your mock exam supply.
In group mode, you are only required to upload some files to verify your score and belonging to your institution; however, because in Group Mode you are providing your mock exam Q&As directly on our site, there is no need to upload mock exam files.

Each group has a group lead (i.e. the student who created the group); ask your group lead to provide you with the group name, which you need in order to join that group.

For verification purposes, we require you to upload both the graded and ungraded versions of the real official exam.

For your mock exam, you must upload questions and your proposed solution to your exam questions as separate files, 1 file for each version.

We are adding this feature, and you will be able to provide Q&As for your mock exam in Single Mode directly from your Xamlinx account.
Supplying a mock exam is not the same as actually uploading (or composing) the exam questions and solutions on our site; supplying a mock exam simply means filling out and submitting the mock exam upload form from your account. The mock exam upload form must be filled out only after you have taken the real official exam, and can be updated later when you have rscore on the real official exam.
Accepting an exam on Xamlinx means that we have reviewed the real exam scores of the students who supplied it, as well as made sure that the mock exam provided by the student(s) is similar but different from the real exam, as well as verifying the student(s)’ institution, checking for correct grammar, and a few other checks that are designed to ensure that we host only high quality mock exams that are similar but different from the official ones.

Cost & Payments

The cost of a mock exam depends on its plan and composition mode (Single Mode or Group Mode).

Xamlinx offers 2 main plans, a standard plan, and a premium plan.

Standard mock exams are composed by high-scoring students and further checked for grammatical and spelling errors, but not reviewed by subject matter specialists.

Premium mock exams are standard mock exams that have been additionally reviewed by subject matter specialists.

Standard Single Mode mock exams are priced at USD 45 each. Standard Group Mode mock exams are priced at USD 75 each. Premium mock exams are priced at USD 100 each regardless of exam composition mode.

For having done well on your real official exam, we are happy to send you a financial reward ranging anywhere from 10 USD to 20 USD per mock exam you provided and that met our acceptance criteria.

As a general rule for mock exams provided in Single Mode:
  • A 1 or 2-page long mock exam with estimated exam duration of less than 2 hours will earn you a USD 10 reward.
  • A 1 or 2-page long mock exam with estimated exam duration of greater than 2 hours will earn you a USD 15 reward.
  • A 2 to 3-page long mock exam with estimated exam duration of greater than 2 hours will earn you a USD 20 reward.

All mock exams provided in Group Mode will earn its composers a USD 12 reward each.

Note: The estimated completion duration on your provided mock exam must match the assigned duration on the real exam, since you are to provide a mock exam of similar difficulty as the real exam.

You will be paid within 48 hours of the time a requester purchased your provided mock exam.
You will be paid via the payment method you added in your account.
All payments made towards a mock exam purchase are final, and non-refundable; however, you will always have the opportunity to view a small sample of the Mock Exam before you make your purchase, and whenever possible, you will be presented with several matches for you to choose from.

In Single Mode, you do not have to upload any file or compose any questions until you are invited to do so! You will be invited to upload or compose your Q&As only when there is at least a 1 in 5 chance that your mock exam will be selected by the requester.

In Group Mode, however, you are required to provide your part of the group’s overall Q&As within 72 hours from the time the group was created; but because you are working in a group, it is less work than it would have been in Single Mode, even though we can’t tell exactly when [or put a percentage on how likely] your group’s mock exam will be selected by a requester.

Exam Integrity

 Students who compose mock exams on our platform are required to also upload a copy of the real exam; this is so that we may ensure that the mock exam composed by the student is different from the real exam. Mock exam composed by students must be their unique and original composition addressing similar topics covered on the real exam, but yet distinct in their contents.
We will not contact your professor unless we have reason to believe that your provided exam score was manufactured. Your professor’s information is requested primarily for automated verification purposes.
In composing your mock exams, although the concepts covered must be the same as those on the official exam you took, the scenarios must be different; you must come up with your own scenarios. Your provided mock exam will be rejected if deemed too similar to the official one.
